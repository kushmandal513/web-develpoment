The Tradition

Where this art comes from

Mithila painting began on the mud walls and courtyards of homes across the Terai and neighbouring Bihar, made by women during weddings and festivals using twigs, brushes and pigments ground from turmeric, sindoor, charcoal and plant leaves. Today the same bold outlines and double-line borders are painted on paper and canvas, but the motifs still carry the meanings they always did.

Machh · Fish

Abundance

The most-painted motif in Mithila art — a wish for a full home and a fertile season.

Mayur · Peacock

Love & monsoon

Painted for weddings, the peacock stands for courtship and the rains that follow it.

Kalpavriksha · Tree

Continuity

A tree that never stops giving — painted for new homes and new generations.

Surya · Sun

Balance

Geometric sun mandalas mark the turning of festivals and the passing of seasons.
